Credit unions vs big banks

Member-first lenders can change the monthly math.

Credit unions are member-owned, so they often return value through competitive loan pricing, lower fees, and relationship-driven eligibility paths instead of optimizing only for outside shareholders. CUB Rates helps surface those lenders next to banks so the difference is easier to inspect.

Profits can flow back to membersMany credit unions use earnings to improve rates, fees, and member services.
Membership may be broader than expectedEmployer, military, regional, local, and donation-based paths can make some credit unions accessible.
Small rate differences compoundA fraction of a percent can become meaningful over years of mortgage payments.
